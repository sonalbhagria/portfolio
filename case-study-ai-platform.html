Overview

The challenge

The Economist Intelligence Unit serves business leaders and policy makers worldwide with deep analytical insight. As the product team sought to modernise its digital platform, the core challenge was clear: how do you design for an audience that demands precision, nuance, and speed — often under significant time pressure?

Existing research methods were slow. Traditional user interviews and usability testing cycles couldn't keep pace with the product roadmap. The team needed a faster, more scalable approach to discovery — without sacrificing depth.

My role

What I led

As Lead Product Designer, I owned the end-to-end design process from discovery through delivery. I partnered closely with product managers, engineers, and senior stakeholders to shape both the design and the research strategy underpinning it.

A key part of my contribution was introducing AI-assisted synthetic user research — a novel approach that dramatically accelerated insight generation during early product discovery.

"How do you design for users you can't always reach? You build a method that brings them closer — even when they're not in the room."

Process

Research & discovery methods

I developed a hybrid research model that combined traditional qualitative methods with emerging AI-assisted techniques — enabling the team to test assumptions earlier and with greater frequency.

01

Synthetic user research

Used AI tools to simulate user personas and generate plausible behavioural hypotheses — validating assumptions before committing to research cycles.

02

User interviews & usability testing

Conducted structured interviews and moderated usability sessions with business analysts, policy researchers, and C-suite audiences across multiple geographies.

03

Insight synthesis

Synthesised findings into actionable design principles and product requirements. Used AI assistants (NotebookLM, Claude) to accelerate thematic analysis.

04

Collaborative design & delivery

Iterated in Figma, aligned with PMs and engineers in agile sprints, and maintained design fidelity through close collaboration during implementation.

Solution

What we built

The redesigned platform centred on reducing cognitive load for a high-expertise audience. Rather than surfacing raw data, the interface was designed to surface synthesised insight — foregrounding the analytical layer that EIU is known for.

Key design decisions included a streamlined navigation architecture, a consistent information hierarchy across insight types, and a modular layout system that could accommodate varied content formats without breaking visual coherence.

Design principles

Guiding decisions

Clarity over density. Policy audiences are information-rich. The platform needed to help them prioritise, not overwhelm.

Trust through consistency. A rigorous design system ensured every interaction felt considered and reliable — reinforcing the EIU brand's authority.

Speed of insight. Layouts, typographic hierarchy, and filtering patterns were all optimised around the moment of insight — getting users to the answer faster.

Outcomes

Key results

↑

Research velocity

Synthetic research methods significantly reduced time-to-insight in early discovery phases, enabling faster iteration on product hypotheses.

✦

Design system adoption

Consistent application of design standards across digital products improved visual coherence and reduced design-engineering handoff friction.

→

Evidence-based decisions

Strengthened integration of user research into product team workflows, supporting more evidence-based roadmap and prioritisation decisions.
