Overview

The challenge

Across the EIU's portfolio of digital products, visual and interaction patterns had evolved organically — resulting in inconsistencies that undermined both user experience and team efficiency. Designers were solving the same problems repeatedly, and engineers were implementing divergent solutions.

The challenge was to create a shared design language — one robust enough to support diverse product types, flexible enough to accommodate evolving content, and adopted widely enough to actually make a difference.

My role

What I led

I led the design, documentation, and adoption of a comprehensive design system — from initial audit through to governance framework. I worked directly with engineering teams to ensure components were implemented faithfully, and with product leadership to establish standards that stuck.

"A design system is only as strong as its adoption. Building it was the first challenge — making teams actually use it was the second."

Process

How we built it

01

UX audit

Conducted a comprehensive audit across all digital products — cataloguing patterns, identifying inconsistencies, and prioritising what to standardise first.

02

Foundation tokens

Established colour, typography, spacing, and elevation tokens — creating a shared vocabulary that both design and engineering teams could reference.

03

Component library

Built a reusable Figma component library with variants, states, and documentation — covering 40+ components from buttons to complex data display patterns.

04

Governance & adoption

Introduced contribution guidelines, review processes, and regular design crits to embed the system into team workflows and ensure ongoing quality.
