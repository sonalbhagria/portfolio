Evolving a traditional search into a multi-turn AI co-pilot

A three-stage design journey — from fixing keyword search, to semantic understanding, to a context-aware decision companion built for global business and policy audiences.

Context & business problem

Search was the primary way users accessed high-value EIU reports and analysis. Users came with clear intent — often looking for specific reports or countries. The content was strong, but the discovery experience hadn't kept pace with scale.

Search accounted for 10%+ of all sessions, making it one of the most critical user journeys on the platform. But research and analytics showed it wasn't meeting expectations around relevance and speed.

The mismatch was structural: users thought in questions and concepts, but the system retrieved through terms and tags. No amount of UI polish could bridge that gap.

This case study documents the three-stage evolution I led — from fixing the foundation, to changing it, to extending it into a multi-turn reasoning companion.

Stage 01

Fixing the foundation

Optimising keyword search — and understanding its limits

What we did

Improving the existing system

  • Refined Elasticsearch logic to improve ranking and reduce noise
  • Redesigned filter experience to improve clarity and terminology
  • Introduced synonyms to account for varied user language
  • Added snippets to all articles for better scanning

What improved

Short-term gains

  • Some reduction in irrelevant results for known queries
  • Slightly better use of filters once terminology was clarified
  • Marginal improvement in search consistency

What still didn't work

The structural limitation

  • Drop-off rate was still 33%
  • Success rate remained unchanged
  • Question-based queries continued to perform poorly
  • Users still depended on knowing how the system worked

Stage 1 — Optimised keyword search results page

"Incremental fixes could not address the core mismatch — between how users think (in questions and exploration) and how the system retrieved information (through terms and tags)."

Stage 02

Changing the foundation

From optimised keyword search to semantic understanding

What we introduced

Semantic search

  • Semantic search to understand intent behind natural-language questions
  • Concept-based relevance instead of strict term matching
  • AI-generated summaries to help users assess result relevance quickly

What this unlocked

Faster, more confident discovery

  • Users could start with questions, not keywords
  • Less time scanning articles to judge relevance
  • Search became a more reliable entry point into high-value content

Design considerations

Interface principles

  • Designed the interface to encourage questions, not single keywords
  • Summaries positioned as entry points, not replacements for source content
  • Clear attribution and links back to original articles reinforced transparency

What this revealed

The next frontier

  • Users still needed to ask follow-up questions manually
  • Context was lost between separate searches
  • Users still had to compare perspectives and reason independently

Stage 2 — Semantic search with AI-generated answer and cited sources

Stage 03

The next evolution

From answering questions to supporting decision journeys

What we observed

After semantic search

  • Users' questions evolved as they learned — each answer raised new, more specific questions
  • Context was lost between searches, forcing users to restate intent repeatedly
  • Users still had to connect insights and reason on their own

Why a multi-turn co-pilot

Real decisions are iterative

  • Real decisions are iterative and exploratory, not single-query events
  • A co-pilot retains context across turns and builds on previous responses
  • Supports follow-up and comparison without losing the thread

Design principles

Transparency & user control

  • Transparency: responses are grounded in sources and assumptions are visible
  • User control: the co-pilot supports reasoning, not conclusions
  • Follow-up question suggestions surface naturally in the interface
  • AI attribution is always visible — users know what generated the answer

Stage 3 — AI Lens: multi-turn decision co-pilot with context-aware follow-up suggestions
